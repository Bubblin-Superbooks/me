He was sitting in our one big chair, smoking, with his legs stretched out in front of him, and although his eyes were half closed, he was watching me constantly. I began to yawn, because I was becoming sleepy. He said he supposed I wanted him to get out. I said no, I didn’t; but my landlady probably did. She didn’t mind our having men callers as long as they went before midnight. It was nearly that now. He said:

“Damn the landlady!” just as he had said, “Damn the new man!” Then he added, “You’re not going to be run by every one, you know.”

I said mischievously:

“Just by you?”

“Just by me,” he replied.

“But when you stay away so long—“

It irritated him for me to refer to that. He said that there were certain matters I wouldn’t understand that had kept him in Richmond, and that he had come as soon as he could.
