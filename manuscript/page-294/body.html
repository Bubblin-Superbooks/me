“You can have the pearls, anyway.”

“But I’d rather have the words,” I stammered, now ashamed of myself, and confused under his look.

“Consider them said, then,” he said, and he laughed. I couldn’t bear him to laugh at me, and I said:

“You don’t mean it. I made you say it, and therefore it has no meaning. I wish it were true.”

“Perhaps it is,” he said.

“Is it?” I demanded eagerly.

“Who knows?” said he.

Lolly came in then. She did not seem at all pleased to see Mr. Hamilton there, and he left soon after. When he was gone, she told me I was a very silly girl to have taken him into my room. I told her I hadn’t; that he had just walked in. Lolly asked me, virtuously, whether I had ever seen her let a man go in there, and I confessed I had not. She wanted to know whether I had told Mr. Hamilton about Dick. Indeed, I had not! The thought of telling him frightened me, and I besought Lolly not to betray me. Also I took off Dick’s ring.
